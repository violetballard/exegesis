# Exegesis

Exegesis is a local-first research and writing workstation for qualitative researchers, educators, and scholarly practitioners.

It is being built for people who work with interviews, field notes, literature, memos, drafts, and analytic fragments, and who need a calmer way to move between evidence, interpretation, and writing. The goal is not to replace scholarly judgment. The goal is to make the workbench around that judgment faster, more traceable, and easier to return to.

## What Exegesis Is

Exegesis combines a writing environment, project notebook, retrieval layer, and AI-assisted drafting workflow into one research-centered workspace.

The product direction includes:

- Local project files and durable project state
- Markdown-first writing and memoing
- Document import and organization
- Research basket workflows for collecting excerpts and context
- Local search and retrieval across project materials
- Drafting and rewrite support with review before apply
- Provenance-aware outputs so generated work remains traceable
- Future qualitative coding, citation, export, OCR, and Zotero workflows

Exegesis is designed for scholarly workflows where context matters and where users need to understand where claims, summaries, and revisions came from.

## Current Status

Exegesis is under active private development.

The current public repository is intentionally minimal while the project moves toward a usable MVP and Community of Practice release. Source code may be published here in curated stages once the internal development infrastructure is separated from distributable product code.

## Design Principles

- **Researcher agency first.** AI should assist reading, drafting, organizing, and revising without hiding decisions from the user.
- **Local-first by default.** Projects should remain understandable and portable outside of any hosted platform.
- **Traceability over magic.** Drafting, retrieval, citation, and rewrite workflows should preserve enough provenance to be inspected later.
- **Writing as analysis.** Exegesis treats writing, memoing, coding, and synthesis as connected parts of scholarly work.
- **Accessible tooling.** The interface should support serious research without assuming a technical background.
